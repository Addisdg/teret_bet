# Content Strategy

## Mission

Teret Bet / ተረት ቤት is an Amharic-first digital story library for Ethiopian
children and Ethiopian diaspora families. The app should help children enjoy
stories, hear warm Amharic language, and build early reading habits in a calm,
illustrated environment.

## Target Audiences

Primary audiences:

* Ethiopian children ages 3-6
* Parents, grandparents, and caregivers reading with children
* Ethiopian diaspora families who want more Amharic at home

Secondary audiences:

* Early childhood teachers
* Community language programs
* Families who want bilingual Amharic/English support later

## Amharic-First Strategy

Teret Bet should treat Amharic as the primary experience, not a translation
afterthought. Story titles, summaries, page text, reader controls, and audio
planning should start from natural Amharic.

English can support parents and future bilingual mode, but the main reading
flow should remain simple, warm, and Amharic-led.

## Ethiopian Diaspora Use Case

Many diaspora families want children to hear and see Amharic regularly, but
need stories that are short, approachable, and easy to read aloud. Teret Bet
should support:

* Short bedtime reading sessions
* Parent-child shared reading
* Offline use during travel
* Future audio narration for children who understand Amharic but cannot yet read
* Familiar cultural warmth without requiring every story to be explicitly Ethiopian

## Copyright-Safe Content Strategy

The app should avoid copying modern copyrighted story text, illustrations, or
adaptations. Public-domain stories can inspire new child-friendly Amharic
versions, but each Teret Bet story should use original wording, softened scenes,
and culturally thoughtful adaptation.

Good source categories:

* Public-domain fables
* Public-domain fairy tales
* Traditional folktale motifs
* Original stories written for Teret Bet

Avoid:

* Copying text from modern children's books
* Copying copyrighted translations
* Copying branded characters, settings, or plot additions from modern media
* Using scary or violent details unchanged when adapting older tales

## Collections

### Ethiopian And African Folktales

These stories can highlight cleverness, kindness, patience, respect, family,
and community. They should be adapted carefully, with sensitivity to regional
and cultural diversity.

### Aesop's Fables

Aesop's fables are useful for short MVP stories because they have clear morals
and simple plots. Adaptations should use natural Amharic, gentle conflict, and
age-appropriate endings.

### Grimm Adaptations

Grimm tales need careful softening. Many original versions include fear,
abandonment, violence, or punishment. Teret Bet versions should preserve the
theme while reducing frightening details.

### Andersen Adaptations

Andersen stories can be beautiful but sometimes sad or complex. Choose stories
carefully and adapt them for emotional safety, clarity, and short page-based
reading.

### World Classics And Folktales

Public-domain classics and folktales from many cultures can broaden the
library. Adaptations should be respectful, simple, and never presented as
authentic cultural education unless reviewed carefully.

## Safety And Cultural Sensitivity

Every story should pass review for:

* Age suitability for children 3-6
* Warm, non-shaming language
* No unnecessary fear, cruelty, or graphic danger
* Respectful family and community portrayals
* Avoidance of ethnic, gender, class, disability, or body stereotypes
* Clear moral without lecturing
* Illustration prompts that avoid frightening imagery

The voice of Teret Bet should feel gentle, encouraging, and rooted in care.
